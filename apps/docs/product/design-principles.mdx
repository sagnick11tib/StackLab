# Design Principles

StackLab should grow under explicit design constraints.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Simulation over decoration" defaultOpen>
    Visuals should clarify behavior. Motion and styling should serve understanding, not distract from it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Tradeoffs over absolutes">
    StackLab should explain why a choice depends on context instead of pretending one pattern wins everywhere.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Original explanation over copied content">
    The platform can be inspired by books and industry ideas, but the published teaching material must stay original.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Production realism over toy examples">
    StackLab should prioritize real systems, meaningful failure cases, and implementation choices that matter in practice.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Modularity over chaos">
    New pages, labs, and templates should fit the product map cleanly and remain easy for contributors to understand.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Documentation as infrastructure">
    Documentation is part of the system. It should encode decisions and support both human and AI contributors.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
